About Me

Hi, I'm Emily Hann, undergraduate student at the University of Pittsburgh studying both anthropology and digital narrative and interactive design. This is my first website using HTML and CSS. I have always been interested in the interaction between humans and the digital world. We are constantly surrounded by digital media and the way we utilize and engage with others using technology is mediated by our cultural upbringing. My goal is to study how we interpret society, culture, and other individuals through the lens of the digital landscape. With this, we can continue to foster new online relationships that foster understanding, cultural exchange, and globalization.

Anthropology

Through cross-cultural analysis and communication, I am qualified to conduct research that focuses on the culturally specific relationships between people, technology, and society. My goal is to not only to understand others and their behaviors and to describe their experiences accurately, but to share and build intercultural relationships that benefit both parties. In this way, I can approach digital design with a cultural lens that appropriately conveys information and enhances user experiences and interaction.

Digital Narrative and Interactive Design

Combining design, narrative, and coding, I am currently acquiring the hard skills required to produce interactive media experiences that use storytelling to convey information through human-computer interfaces. I have experience in programming languages Python, HTML, and CSS and editing software such as Audacity and Gimp, which I have used to create a variety of media for class projects. In combination with my anthropology degree, I hope to create rich cultural narratives through digital media that enhance cultural awareness and foster diversity, inclusion, and understanding within various communities.

Japanese

I have successfully completed four semesters of intensive Japanese and hundreds of hours of individual study. As a complimnet to my Asian Studies Certificate, learning Japanese not only allows me to communicate effectively with other Japanese speakers, but also introduced me to cultural concepts that exist only within Japan. As such, I have a specialized academic concentation in Japan and Japanese culture and East Asian cultures more broadly.
